    • The Autonomous car project aimed to build and program an autonomous car that follows a pre-defined line and detects obstacles using a Raspberry Pi 4. This endeavor required integrating mechanical assembly, electronic circuitry, sensor deployment, and software programming to craft a fully autonomous vehicle. The team focused on constructing the robotic car using a chassis kit, ensuring all parts were securely assembled, and mounting various sensors for line tracking and obstacle avoidance. The software development aspect utilized the PIGPIO library for GPIO pin management and involved creating a weight-style algorithm to adjust the carâ€™s steering based on line alignment data. A crucial part of the project was the integration of five line-tracking sensors and an ultrasonic sensor, which were calibrated to enhance the vehicle's obstacle detection capabilities.

    Challenges such as motor control issues and sonar sensor inconsistencies were significant hurdles during the project. Motor responsiveness varied due to power supply issues and encoder feedback inaccuracies, requiring adjustments in the control software and hardware setups. The sonar sensor occasionally delivered erratic readings influenced by environmental factors, necessitating robust filtering and debouncing algorithms to integrate these readings into the control logic effectively.

    Despite these challenges, the project succeeded in creating a functional autonomous line-following car. The integration of hardware and software was a major success, highlighted by the effective sensor setup and the precision of the steering controls developed. The project not only demonstrated the technical capabilities of Team ControlAltDelete but also provided them with invaluable lessons in robotics, emphasizing the importance of adaptive problem-solving and thorough system testing.
