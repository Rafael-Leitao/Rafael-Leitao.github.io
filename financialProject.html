    • For my final project in the Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate, I developed a comprehensive analysis of a cyclist company using Tableau. This project was the culmination of the skills and techniques I acquired throughout the course, which included data cleaning, visualization, and interpretation. Utilizing the APPAS (Ask, Prepare, Process, Analyze, and Share) methodology, a structured approach recommended by Google, I was able to gather, process, and visualize the company's data, providing insightful and actionable conclusions.

    The data was visualized in several key dashboards, where I employed line charts, bar charts, and pie charts to clearly communicate the trends and insights drawn from the dataset.

    One of the significant insights uncovered was the difference in usage patterns between casual users and members. For instance, members consistently had higher ride volumes compared to casual users, especially during peak hours like 8 AM and 5 PM, which align with typical commuting times. Additionally, the data showed a strong preference for classic bikes among casual users, whereas members exhibited a more balanced distribution between classic and electric bikes. This type of analysis helps the company identify the differing needs and behaviors of their user base, potentially guiding marketing and service improvements.

    Overall, this project demonstrated my ability to conduct thorough data analysis and visualization. By following the APPAS methodology, I was able to provide a clear and impactful presentation of the cyclist company's data. This project not only served as the conclusion of my Google Data Analytics certificate but also reinforced my confidence in using Tableau as a powerful tool for data-driven decision-making.
